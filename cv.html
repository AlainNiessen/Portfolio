Curriculum Vitae

Let's talk about my studies and professionnal career

Every person has their own unique story. Here is a glimpse into mine.

After a long career in the social sector as social worker, I decided in 2019 to study Webdevelopper and to follow my passion to Web technologies. During this phase, I have begun to incorporate different technologies to create responsive, user-friendly and high performance Websites.

I enjoy taking complex challenges, experimenting with different technologies to solve problems or to level-up the workflow and turning this work to simple and beauty Websites. I also love the logic and structure of coding and always strive to write elegant and efficient code.

When I'm not coding, you will find me walking around in nature or shooting pics. Yes, photography is my second big passion.

My professional career

This section gives you the opportunity to discover more about my professional career and acquired skills that are important for the profession as Web developer. Feel free to look around.

2003 - 2019

Professional career as social pedagogue

During this time I worked in various social institutions and under various contracts (full-time, part-time, fixed-term, permanent, ...).

Description

The focus was on accompanying people in their socio-professional reintegration. For several years I worked in a social-therapeutic residential community with people in very different problem situations. The work there focused on individual support, group dynamics, mediation and conflict management. I also worked for several years in a service that helped mentally ill people get back into working life. Job acquisition and communication with employers were also an important part of this work. During this time I also offered supervision for the employees of physically ill people to better understand the illness and its accompanying symptoms

For another 3 years I worked in a facility that offered various studios for the preparation of furniture and household items. In these studios I was in charge of carpentry and restoration and accompanied various people there (young unemployed people, asylum seekers, people with a disability, ...). I was able to gain further experience in smaller jobs, for example I worked in an asylum seekers home for a while, where I gave lessons in computer science (Word and Excel) and organized sporting activities, or accompanied young people in schools in Erasmus + projects.

Acquired skills

Over the years I have been able to gain experience in communication, teamwork, mediation, conflict management, file management, goal and solution-oriented work, individual advice, group management, supervision, administration (statistics, writing reports, bookkeeping), and working with other services.

You can find a detailed list of the various positions on my LinkedIn profile.
